# Energy & Climate Disclosure — Wisconsin Firms

Evaluation of ESG disclosure completeness and credibility across 20 Wisconsin-based companies using GRI-aligned energy (302) and emissions (305) indicators.

## Key Insight (Don’t Skip This)

Across firms, ESG disclosure completeness is extremely low (<15% of indicators reported), with most sectors showing near-zero coverage.  

This reveals a significant **transparency gap** between reported ESG commitments and actual disclosure practices.

## Key Findings

- Disclosure is concentrated in basic metrics (energy use, Scope 1), while critical indicators (Scope 3, renewable sourcing) are largely missing  
- Third-party assurance is inconsistent, weakening data credibility  
- Larger firms do **not** consistently disclose more complete ESG data  
- Reporting patterns suggest **strategic disclosure** (legitimacy-driven) rather than full transparency

## Why it matters

ESG disclosures are widely used in investment, policy, and corporate decision-making—yet this analysis shows they may systematically underrepresent risk.

Implications:
- ESG data may be unreliable for benchmarking or investment decisions  
- Stronger standards (TCFD, ISSB) are needed for comparability and accountability  
- Misalignment with regional climate transition goals creates policy and governance risks

## Methods

- Content analysis of 20 firms (2022–2024)
- Binary scoring of GRI-aligned indicators (0/1 → disclosure completeness index)
- Sector-level aggregation and comparison
- Correlation analysis (revenue vs disclosure completeness)
